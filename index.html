Price comparisons use the same match level as the rest of the dashboard. At Level 0 the items are physically identical (same barcode), so gaps are directly comparable. At broader levels pack sizes and flavours can differ, so gaps partly reflect size differences.

Pet Valu price position vs each retailer

A pair is one Pet Valu SKU matched to a competitor listing with a valid price on both sides. When pack size can be read from both listings, prices are compared per unit (per g / ml / count) and the competitor price is size-adjusted to the Pet Valu pack, so multi-packs and different bag sizes compare fairly. When a Pet Valu SKU matches several competitor listings, the reference is the median. Walmart, Loblaws, and Amazon 1P are matched by exact barcode only, so their numbers are identical at every level.

How product matching works

Every product listing from Pet Valu, PetSmart, Chewy, and Amazon.ca is compared using four match strictness levels, ordered from strictest to broadest.

Level 0 requires an identical barcode. Level 1 matches the same brand, product line, and flavor at any pack size. Level 2 relaxes flavor and size — same product line, any variant or pack size. Level 3 compares at the brand-and-format level when product lines differ.

Strictest — identical product
Broadest — same-brand comparable

How strictness works

Levels are nested, not independent. Selecting a broader strictness automatically includes every stricter match. Choosing Level 2 counts SKUs that match at Level 0, Level 1, or Level 2 — you cannot mix arbitrary combinations.

  • Broader levels automatically include every stricter match.
  • Stricter levels mean higher confidence the products are the same item.
  • Broader levels capture more assortment overlap across retailers.
